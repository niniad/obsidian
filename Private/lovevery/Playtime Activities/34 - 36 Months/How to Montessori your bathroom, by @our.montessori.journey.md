**Handwashing:** cut a bar of soap in half or choose a soap dispenser that is easy for your child to use. **Storage:** storing washcloths and towels in a drawer or basket under the sink allows your child to gather what they need for bath time or washing their face. To give them a sense of pride and ownership in their routine, invite your child to help you launder, fold, and put these items away.

### Toileting

![Potty seat and a basket of books next to a bathtub in a bathroom](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/Montessori-Bathroom-3.jpg?resize=1600%2C2000&ssl=1)

**Potty/Toilet:** for toileting independence, provide a [potty seat](https://www.amazon.com/BABYBJORN-Toilet-Trainer-White-Turquoise/dp/B077GBJB9H?ref_=ast_sto_dp&th=1) for the toilet with a low step stool, or a [potty](https://www.amazon.com/Bjorn-Potty-Chair-Powder-Yellow/dp/B07X6WLT91?ref_=ast_sto_dp&th=1) chair. Ensure that the toilet paper is within reach, and model how to dispense it using slow hands, so your child can follow your movements.

**Basket:** store books and wipes in a basket. If your toddler is still learning to use the potty, include a change of underwear and clothes.

**Mirror:** mount a full-length (child safe) [acrylic mirror](https://www.amazon.com/Acrylic-Plastic-Finished-Polished-H-C/dp/B08FZRQKKL/ref=sr_1_4?dchild=1&keywords=acrylic+mirror&qid=1602033465&sr=8-4) at their level to help promote body awareness and aid in self-dressing.

### **The bath**

![Young child putting towels away in a drawer in a bathroom](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/Montessori-Bathroom-5.jpg?resize=2000%2C1333&ssl=1)

**Washing:** [travel-size bottles](https://www.amazon.com/Approved-Uerstar-Silicone-Cosmetic-Containers/dp/B07DQJBFQ7/ref=sr_1_3?dchild=1&keywords=travel+size+bottle&qid=1602033514&sr=8-3) for shampoo and body wash are the perfect size for little hands. Only fill them with as much as they need at a time. A bar of soap can work well, too.

**Toys:** just as you would in a Montessori play space, limit the number of [bath toys](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-inspector) available at one time and rotate them to keep your child interested. [The Liquid Lab](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-investigator) is a fun way to practice pouring and transferring water. The [pitcher](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-realist) is also an ideal tool for rinsing hair üòâ

**Low hook**: mount a [low hook](https://www.amazon.com/Command-Bathroom-Water-Resistant-Strips-2-Strip/dp/B000IN1I5A/ref=sr_1_4?dchild=1&keywords=3m+hook+bathroom&qid=1602033636&sr=8-4) so your child knows where to hang up their towel each time they are done with their bath.

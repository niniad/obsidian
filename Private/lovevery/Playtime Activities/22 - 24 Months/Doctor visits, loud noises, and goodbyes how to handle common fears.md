## How can you encourage your toddler to be less clingy?

As your toddler ventures out into the world, they may need lots of reassurance. Clinging to you at a birthday party or play group is a sign that they‚Äôre seeking an internal sense of security and safety.

![](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/18_B.jpg?resize=1024%2C607&ssl=1)

As someone that they trust, you are their **secure base**, a place that they can come back to as often as needed. At times, this may feel a bit frustrating‚Äîmaybe you‚Äôre craving some adult time or feel embarrassed that your toddler is the only one not joining the group. In these moments, by staying close and being a calm, steady presence, you‚Äôre actually helping your toddler learn to become more independent in the future.

## Child care drop-offs: tips for easier goodbyes

When your toddler has separation anxiety, dropping them off at child care is much easier said than done. Your toddler‚Äôs crying and clinging can trigger a lot of emotions and your protective instincts. Some children have an easier time adjusting than others, but most will struggle with separating from you at some point. If you‚Äôre experiencing difficult drop-offs, know that your toddler will likely calm down within five minutes or less after you leave. Here are some strategies that can help ease your toddler‚Äôs anxiety when it‚Äôs time to say goodbye ‚ù§Ô∏è

### 1. Give them a sense of confidence

Showing your toddler that you trust their childcare providers is one of the best ways to ease their anxiety. Research shows that when toddlers are uncertain, they look to their caregivers to see how they should feel‚Äîknown as ‚Äúsocial referencing.‚Äù When you drop off your toddler, greet the people in the space with a smile, and take a moment to chat with the provider before you leave, if you can.

### 2. Resist the urge to sneak out

It can be tempting to make a quick exit when your toddler isn‚Äôt looking, especially if they seem happy. Even though it could trigger tears, saying goodbye helps your toddler build trust with you and their child care provider, and build positive associations with their child care space.

### 3. Tell them when you‚Äôll be back

Your toddler doesn‚Äôt have a concept of time yet, but they do have a concept of routines. Timing your return to a specific part of their daily routine will give them a clearer expectation of when you‚Äôll return. Let your toddler know, ‚ÄúI‚Äôll be back right after nap time today, and then we‚Äôll go home together.‚Äù

Your toddler‚Äôs child care provider can reinforce this during the day. If your child starts asking for you, the provider can say, ‚ÄúYou really miss Daddy right now. Daddy always comes back after nap time! He‚Äôll be so excited to see you, too.‚Äù

### 4. Have a short and sweet goodbye ritual

Keep your goodbye simple and consistent, and involve your toddler‚Äôs care provider if you can. ‚ÄúI‚Äôm going to go to work now. Charlie is going to take such good care of you while I‚Äôm at work! I can‚Äôt wait to read a story with you when I get back.‚Äù Leave with confidence, knowing that this is a great opportunity for the child care provider to comfort your toddler as they build their bond with each other.

### 5. Linger on the reunion

At pick-up time, you may feel rushed to get home. When you can, reconnect with your toddler in at the child care space. They may be excited to see you, distracted by an activity, or even tearful, expressing some delayed grief at your absence. Take a few minutes to savor the hugs, sit on the floor and play, or comfort them. Let your toddler know, ‚ÄúI really missed you today, and I‚Äôm so glad we‚Äôre back together.‚Äù

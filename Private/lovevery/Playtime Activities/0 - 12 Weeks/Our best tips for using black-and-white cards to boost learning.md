### 4. Slowly move a card or toy to develop a new skill

Your baby is getting better at visual tracking, or following a moving object with their eyes. Try holding a dark or mostly dark object‚Äîa Black & White Card, a [Black & White Mitten, the Silicone Rattle with Removable Ball](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-looker), or even a household object like a dark lid to a jar‚Äîabout 12 inches away from your baby‚Äôs face and moving it slowly back and forth. As your baby gets better at following the object with their eyes, you can try moving it farther away.

Place your baby in different positions for tracking practice: lying on their back, in tummy time, or hold them upright. Try moving the black-and-white image up and to the right or left so your baby needs to turn their neck and tilt it back to see. This movement works their eye muscles and stretches and strengthens their neck at the same time.

### 5. Talk about what they see

In the early weeks, your newborn could only process one sensory stream at a time; looking and listening simultaneously was overwhelming. At 2 months, they‚Äôre ready for more. When you shake the Silicone Rattle with Removable Ball or name the images on the [Black & White Universe Cards](https://lovevery.com/products/black-and-white-cards?variant=39773270048901) or the [Black & White Ocean Cards](https://lovevery.com/products/black-and-white-cards?variant=39773269983365), your baby can starting pairing what they hear with what they see.

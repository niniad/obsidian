![](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/2021_29_09_LOOKBOOK0087_1.jpg?fit=2048%2C1214&ssl=1)

There‚Äôs often a lot of excitement when your baby learns a new major skill, like rolling over, sitting up, or saying their first word. But to get there, they first need to develop a series of more subtle **micro skills**.

**Micro skill #1. Turning their head fully to each side as they lie on their back.** Many babies prefer to turn their head to one side or the other. Try putting the [Standing Card Holder](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-looker)with a [Black & White Card](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-looker) on your baby‚Äôs preferred side. Entice your baby to look straight up by placing them directly under [The Mobile](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-looker) with the black-and-white side facing down.

**Micro skill #3. Lifting their head and keeping it centered in tummy time.** While your baby may be lifting their head at an angle, lifting and holding it centered is a bigger challenge. **Micro skill #1. **Micro skill #3. ### Milestone: Babbling

From birth, babies experiment with making different sounds and noises. Over time, they develop more complex vocalizations, including cooing, gurgling, and, eventually, babbling. Those first ‚Äúma-ma-ma‚Äù or ‚Äúpa-pa-pa‚Äù repetitions‚Äîspecifically known as ‚Äúcanonical babbling‚Äù‚Äîusually start between 5 and 9 months. **Micro skill #1. **Micro skill #3. Cooing vowel sounds.** Your baby‚Äôs first coos can be just as exciting as their first babbles. While you may hear grunts and squeaks in the first months, most babies begin cooing vowel sounds around 3 months or later.

## Tummy time at 0‚Äì2 months

At this stage, the goal is for your baby to become comfortable on their belly. In tummy time, your newborn or young baby may:

![](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/image.png?resize=720%2C444&ssl=1)

* Round their back slightly
* Bend their arms and tuck them close to the body with hands near shoulders
* Bend their knees under their hips

![Tummy time at 0-2 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-2-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Mostly have their head down
* Be able to lift and turn their head briefly with great effort

![Tummy time at 0-2 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-3-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Make crawling or pushing motions with their feet
* Be comfortable with [either cheek down](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/head-turning-dont-miss-this-expert-mamas-essential-newborn-tip/)

## Tummy time at 3‚Äì5 months

At this stage, the goal is strengthening head, neck, and upper body muscles. In tummy time, your baby may:

![Tummy time at 3-5 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-4-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Straighten their legs so that their lower belly touches the surface beneath them
* Use their elbows for stability, either under or in front of their shoulders

![Tummy time at 3-5 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-5-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Lift their head to a 45-degree angle and hold it briefly without bobbing, getting closer to 90 degrees by 5 months of age

![Tummy time at 3-5 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-6-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Turn their lifted head to fully look left, right, and straight ahead
* Turn their head to place the opposite cheek down

## Tummy time at 5‚Äì7 months

At this stage, the goal is for your baby to continue to strengthen their muscles and grasp [nearby toys](https://lovevery.com/pages/tummy-time-toys). In tummy time, your baby may:

![Tummy time at 5-7 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-7-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Press through their forearms to lift their upper chest
* Begin to press up briefly through straight arms

![Tummy time at 5-7 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-8-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Lift their arms and legs off the floor and rock back and forth, or appear to be ‚Äúswimming‚Äù or ‚Äúflying‚Äù

![Tummy time at 5-7 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-9-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Use open hands to reach for, then eventually grab and play with [enticing toys](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser) placed immediately nearby
* Bring their knees under their hips briefly or dig their feet into the floor in an alternating pattern

## Tummy time at 6‚Äì10 months

At this stage, the goal for your baby is movement. Your older baby may:

![Tummy time at 6-10 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-10-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Belly [crawl](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/helping-your-baby-crawl-and-why-its-important-to-keep-them-crawling/) to move forward or backward

![Tummy time at 6-10 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-11-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Get up fully onto their hand and knees
* Do planks on their knees or feet, lifting their belly and hips slightly off the floor

![Tummy time at 6-10 months](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/US-I-Blog-I-Tummy-Time-Stages-12-copy.webp?resize=1000%2C617&ssl=1)

* Transition from their belly to a sitting position, often using their hands and knees

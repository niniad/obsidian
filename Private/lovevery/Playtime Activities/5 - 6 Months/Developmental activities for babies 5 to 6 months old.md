### Build self-feeding skills with The Magic Tissue Box

![Baby playing with the Magic Tissue box by Lovevery](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/EV_6_Nekole_SupportingSittingandReaching_Screenshot.jpg?resize=1024%2C1024&ssl=1)

Look Inside [The Senser Play Kit](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser): Magic Tissue Box

Before babies are ready to start feeding themselves, they must be able to sit up on their own. Try this simple activity to help your baby develop the strength and balance to sit unassisted:

1. Place your baby in a seated position on the floor, supported between your legs.
2. Set the [Magic Tissue Box](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser) in front of them.
3. Encourage them to pull out and explore the tissues.

As your baby leans forward and reaches for the tissues, they work their core muscles, improve their balance, and develop bilateral hand coordination (the ability to use both hands at once) üôÇ

### Laundry basket and ribbons

![Baby looking up at streamers coming out of a laundry hamper](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/2021_08_25_App0378-e1635200044948.jpg?resize=8192%2C4853&ssl=1)

### Kicking play with the Rainbow Spinner

![Baby kicking the Spinning Rainbow from The Senser Play Kit by Lovevery](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/EV_6_Giselle_FootPlayWithTheSpinningRainbow_Screenshot.jpg?resize=1024%2C1024&ssl=1)

Look Inside [The Senser Play Kit](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser): Rainbow Spinner

Around 5 months old, your baby starts to realize that their legs and feet are part of their body. This kicking activity helps them learn their body can create noise and movement.

1. Position the [Rainbow Spinner](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser) close enough so that your baby can make contact with it when they extend their legs.
2. Guide one of your baby‚Äôs feet to the spinner and show them how they can kick to make it turn. Then, let them try on their own.
3. The repetitive clicking sound and spinning movement give your baby incentive to keep going.

### Increase side-lying stamina with a book

![Baby touching the Parts of Me board book by Lovevery](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/EV_Rachel-Coley_Side-Lying_SCREENSHOT.jpg?resize=1024%2C1024&ssl=1)

Side-lying is still an important play position at this age because it builds core strength and balance. It also presents an opportunity for your baby to bring their hands to midline, reach in front of them, and prepare to roll ‚ù§Ô∏è

1. Use the [‚ÄòParts of Me‚Äô Book](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser)¬†to hold your baby‚Äôs attention as they play on their side.
2. If they need some help to stay steady, support their back with your hand, legs, or a rolled-up blanket.
3. As you read and point to the pictures, your baby may reach across their body to touch the page; this movement will help them get comfortable with rolling onto their tummy if they‚Äôre still working on this skill.

### Engage your baby with a sensory bag

![Baby playing with a sensory bag filled with ice](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/2021_08_25_App0523-e1635197819855.jpg?resize=5468%2C4874&ssl=1)

You can repurpose zip-lock bags for all kinds of mess-free sensory activities. Just fill one up with ice and a little bit of water, then tape the edges to the floor or a table with painter‚Äôs tape.

Other items to use in sensory bags:

* Hair gel and pom poms
* Oil, water, and food coloring
* Flour, water, and food coloring
* Pumpkin seeds
* Water and water beads
* Paint

### Mesmerize your baby with a sensory bottle

![Baby doing tummy time and looking at a water bottle filled with different colors](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Fun-Developmental-Activities_Body-3.jpg?resize=1176%2C784&ssl=1)

Your baby will love staring at different colors and watching various items move as they grasp and roll a sensory bottle.

Fill a clear plastic bottle with water, oil, and food coloring and show your baby how the colors shift as you move it. You can glue the lid to the bottle if you want a little extra security.

Other items to use in a [sensory bottle](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/5-easy-sensory-bottle-ideas/):

* Different types and colors of beads, buttons, or pom poms
* Uncooked pasta
* Mini plastic eggs, water, and glitter
* Water and water beads
* Uncooked rice and beans

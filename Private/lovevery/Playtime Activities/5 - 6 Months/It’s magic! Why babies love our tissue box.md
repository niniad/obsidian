## 5 ways to play with the Magic Tissue Box:

### **Object permanence**

Use the tissues to partially hide other playthings, like the Soft Cotton Rainbow Ball from [The Play Kits](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser). Does your baby try to find it? This is an early lesson in object permanence, or the idea that even if something disappears for a while, it still exists. You can help your baby understand this concept by narrating as you play: ‚ÄúWhere is the ball? Do you see it anywhere? Oh! There it is‚Äîit‚Äôs under the tissue!‚Äù

### **Tactile exploration**

![Baby playing with Magic Tissues from Lovevery](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/2021_05_12_Blog1767-e1626884275890.jpg?resize=2000%2C1334&ssl=1)

In photo: Magic Tissues from [The Senser Play Kit](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-senser) in the Treasure Basket from [The Inspector Play Kit](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits-the-inspector)

Your baby gains feedback about the world through touch receptors in their fingertips. Place the colorful tissues in a basket or on a tray on the floor in front of them for free exploration during tummy time or in a supported sitting position.

### **Grasping and reaching**

In tummy time, if your baby is grasping, place the box on its side and encourage your baby to try to pull the tissues out. As they develop this skill, you can add a challenge by moving the tissue box away. This encourages your baby to stretch, reach, and push off, exercising the muscles in their neck, shoulders and back.

### **Peekaboo**

![Mother playing with the Magic Tissue Box with their baby](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/2021_05_12_Blog1744.jpg?resize=1024%2C683&ssl=1)

Research shows you can make a difference in your baby‚Äôs ability to focus with a shared activity. This can be as simple as taking turns with your baby to pull Magic Tissues out of the box.

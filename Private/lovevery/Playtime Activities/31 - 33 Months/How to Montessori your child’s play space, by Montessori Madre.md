## A Montessori play space promotes the following key elements:

### Independence

![Montessori inspired play area with rug, book case, table, and more](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/IMG_0004.jpg?resize=3941%2C2956&ssl=1)

The Montessori method encourages independence from a young age to help children become more capable and make their own discoveries.Easily accessible materials, toys, and books encourage your child to gravitate toward their interests, giving them autonomy over their learning experience. Rotate the selection periodically, keeping favorites available as you introduce fresh playthings.

### Freedom of Movement

![Montessori inspired play area with a shelf filled with toys by Lovevery](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/IMG_0007-copy.jpg?resize=2896%2C3862&ssl=1)

In photo: Playthings from [The Play Kits](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits)

Give your child plenty of uncluttered floor space and opportunities to climb, jump, push, pull, step up and down, and throw. Physical challenges like these can help your child develop key skills during their sensitive period for movement.

Bonus tip: Children love to see themselves as they move‚Äîadding a large, securely mounted mirror to your space can motivate and inspire them.

### Order

![Lovevery toys on different shelves on a wooden bookcase](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/IMG_0010-copy.jpg?resize=2966%2C3955&ssl=1)

In photo: Playthings from [The Play Kits](https://lovevery.com/products/the-play-kits)

Children learn best in a clutter-free environment with a limited number of toys available at one time. Order helps your child home in on things that interest them and promotes focused attention. An easy way to encourage order is to categorize materials into trays, baskets, or shallow containers. Periodically rotate the selection, keeping favorites available as you introduce fresh playthings.

### Beauty

Just like adults, children gravitate toward what is beautiful. For this reason, you may see many objects in a Montessori space that use materials like wood, cotton, and wool. A neutral color palette and natural lighting can help make the environment feel natural and beautiful. You can hang artwork at your child‚Äôs eye level. Including a potted plant, which your 2-year-old can also learn to water and take care of over time, is a simple way to add beauty‚Äîjust be sure to keep it out of reach.

### Personality

![Wooden book holder with stepping stool](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/IMG_0004-2-e1598893559423.jpg?resize=1665%2C2000&ssl=1)

Montessori emphasizes meaningful objects and artwork that tell a story or bring you joy. You can, for example, frame textiles and prints connected to your culture, choose art highlighting your travels, display your 2-year-old‚Äôs favorite family photo, or hang a piece of artwork they recently created. These detailed elements personalize the space while emphasizing what your family values most, giving it an extra special touch of beauty and meaning ‚ù§Ô∏è

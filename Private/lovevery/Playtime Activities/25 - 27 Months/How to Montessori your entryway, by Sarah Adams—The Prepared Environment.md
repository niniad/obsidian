Dr. Maria Montessori coined the term ‚Äúprepared environment‚Äù to describe orderly, child-friendly spaces that encourage confidence and independence. Creating this kind of environment at home doesn‚Äôt have to be stressful or expensive; simple tweaks to your existing space can make it even more inviting for your child.

## Here are a few ways you can prepare your entryway to be child-friendly:

**Low hooks for coats**

![Two hanging jackets and a mirror next to the front door](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/IMG_9117.jpg?resize=1200%2C711&ssl=1)

Getting ready to leave the home allows your child to learn many practical life skills, like

choosing a jacket or coat suitable for the weather. Having the appropriate items close by will encourage your child to practice self dressing as much as possible.

**Somewhere to sit and a designated place for shoes and boots**

![Young child putting on rain boots while sitting on the stairs](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/image2-Edit.jpg?resize=1200%2C711&ssl=1)

If you can, place a child-sized chair, stool, or bench in the entryway, where your child can sit while they practice [putting on their shoes](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/a-2-year-old-friendly-guide-to-putting-on-shoes-and-socks/). If space is limited, the bottom step of a staircase or a soft rug can work just as well.

![Cabinets and drawers in an entry way with an open spot for shoes](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/IMG_7412.jpg?resize=1200%2C711&ssl=1)

A tray, basket, or rack, where your child can keep their shoes, helps maintain order in the space and reinforces routines. Remember that less is more in Montessori; [limited choices](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/why-fewer-choices-help-your-child-establish-independence/) in the space help your child practice decision-making. For example, choosing between two pairs of shoes is less overwhelming than having to decide among more.

**A container or drawer for seasonal items**

Keeping a special place to store seasonal items such as hats, umbrellas, and sunglasses is a great chance to talk about different kinds of weather and the gear your child needs to be ready for it. Stowing winter boots may also help reduce the potential for a power struggle when your two-year-old decides to wear them in July üôÉ

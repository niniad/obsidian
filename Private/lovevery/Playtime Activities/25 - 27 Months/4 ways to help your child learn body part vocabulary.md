# 4 ways to help your child learn body part vocabulary

Source: https://blog.lovevery.com/language-communication/teaching-toddler-body-part-names/

![Little boy sitting on the couch pointing at their nose](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Leo_225.jpg?fit=2048%2C1214&ssl=1)

Research shows children may be able to identify as many as 13 different body parts by the age of 2. If your toddler can already point to their nose or show you their hands, they may be ready to move on to trickier terms. Toddlers typically learn the names of their basic facial features, stomach, hands, and feet, before less commonly talked about parts, like their eyebrows, fingers, and elbows.

Learning the names of their own parts tends to be easier for children than learning the names of another person‚Äôs. To help bridge the gap, try labeling their body part (‚ÄúWhere are your eyebrows?‚Äù) and then someone else‚Äôs (‚ÄúCan you point to Daddy‚Äôs eyebrows?‚Äù).

### 3. Use humor to get them talking

Your toddler likely knows many more body part words than they can actually say. To work on their expressive vocabulary, put a shoe on your hand and say, ‚ÄúWait, does this go on my hand?

## It‚Äôs hard‚Äîand exciting‚Äîto be 2: advice for navigating this underrated stage

### What‚Äôs behind your 2-year-old‚Äôs challenging behavior?

**They‚Äôre trying to figure out how to communicate.** Your 2-year-old‚Äôs vocabulary may be growing rapidly, but they still have many thoughts and feelings that they can‚Äôt put into words. ### How to help your 2-year-old thrive during this exciting yet tricky time

#### Let them help

Your toddler wants to be involved. They learn from watching and imitating you and from trying out new skills themselves. When you prepare food or fold laundry, consider inviting your 2-year-old to participate with a task of their own, like putting fruit in a bowl or matching socks. These experiences can make the difference between a meltdown and a moment of pride and connection.

#### Make time and space for them to explore

Your toddler quickly absorbs new information and ideas through everyday experiences and play. When you can, try to slow down and minimize expectations. Some free, unstructured time can encourage your child to try new skills or dive deeper into exploration.

#### Give them a chance to try

It can be tempting to say, ‚ÄúBe careful!‚Äù and swoop in when your child wants to climb a new playground structure. Many 2-year-olds are surprisingly capable. Stay close for safety, but let your 2-year-old see what they can do. They‚Äôll get more agile and confident with practice.

#### Just be there

So much of your toddler‚Äôs challenging behavior is out of their control, and may even be scary and overwhelming to them. They need your love and comfort more than ever as they explore new emotions and take small steps toward independence ‚ù§Ô∏è

Angeline S. Lillard is a Professor of Psychology at the University of Virginia, specializing in child development, education, Montessori, and pretend play. Her research focuses on the impact of pretend play on children's development, with publications in journals such as *Psychological Bulletin*. She investigates the cognitive and social aspects of play, including its role in social understanding, emotion regulation, and executive function.

## What are Montessori toys?

Any plaything can be a ‚Äú[Montessori toy](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/what-are-montessori-toys/)‚Äù if it aligns with the Montessori learning philosophy of purposeful play. Montessori classrooms emphasize simple playthings made of natural materials that allow young children to practice one skill at a time. Uncomplicated in construction and purpose, these kinds of materials feel good to hold, don‚Äôt provide excess stimulation like bright lights and loud noises, and tap into your child‚Äôs natural inclination to learn.

Learn more about what makes something a ‚Äú[Montessori toy](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/what-are-montessori-toys/).‚Äù

## What are the best Montessori [toys for 2-year-olds](https://lovevery.com/pages/toys-for-two-year-olds)?

### A visual timer for tracking the passage of time

![Child setting the Countdown Color Timer from The Investigator Play Kit by Lovevery](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/2020.5.19-Blog-day17118-1.jpg?resize=1024%2C683&ssl=1)

Transitions are often difficult for a 2-year-old, because the end or beginning of something can take them by surprise. **Ideas for play:**

* Introduce the timer to your child when they‚Äôre calm and let them play with it, encouraging them to be gentle.

* Though your child is still years away from truly understanding how time passes, you can introduce the concept now. * Even if you‚Äôre not timing something, you can watch the timer together to help your child calm down. Talk about how they‚Äôre feeling: ‚ÄúIt looks like you‚Äôre upset and angry; let‚Äôs watch the timer and breathe together‚Äînow I see you‚Äôre feeling a little calmer‚Äù

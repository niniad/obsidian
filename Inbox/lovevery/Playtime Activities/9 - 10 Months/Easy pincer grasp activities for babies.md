The pincer grasp‚Äîa coordinated pinch of the thumb and index finger that allows your baby to pick up and hold a small object‚Äîis actually an indication of both cognitive and fine motor development. ## When babies learn the pincer grasp

Babies typically develop the pincer grasp between 10 and 12 months, although it can happen earlier or later.¬†Before then, the primary ways your baby picked something up were the palmar grasp (a newborn reflex) and the raking grasp (about 6 months).

This fine motor ability will help with many future tasks, from [getting dressed](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/more-tips-to-help-children-learn-to-get-dressed/) to holding a crayon.

## Pincer grasp activities for babies

The pincer grasp is a skill your child will need to [practice well into their toddler (and even preschool) years](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/fact-the-pincer-grasp-is-not-just-for-babies/). Here are two easy play ideas for babies.

![Mother their child sit up while playing with a whisk filled with pom poms](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/Lovevery_Greyson5787-Edit.20211013185646445.jpg?resize=1024%2C683&ssl=1)

### Pom-pom whisk challenge

This activity gives your baby a chance to develop the dexterity they‚Äôll need later to button, zip, draw, and write ‚ù§Ô∏è

* Push pom-poms between the wires of a whisk.
* Encourage your child to use their pincer grasp to pull them out.
* Let them hold the whisk in one hand as they remove the pom-poms with the other to work on bilateral coordination.
* Consider placing a container nearby so your baby can [put the pom-poms in it](https://blog.lovevery.com/playtime-activities/pom-pom-and-pitcher-sensory-bin/).

Please supervise your baby at all times during this activity and ensure they do not mouth the pom-poms. Small items like these could become choking hazards. Put the pom-poms safely away when play is done.

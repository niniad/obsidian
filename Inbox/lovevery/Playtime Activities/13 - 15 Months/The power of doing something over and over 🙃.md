## A simple way to increase your toddler‚Äôs attention span

When you read a book or practice stacking blocks with your toddler, you do more than bond with them. You also help increase their attention span **‚ù§Ô∏è**

Two related studies published in 2019 and 2022 found that when parents and their toddlers focused on a toy together, the toddlers paid attention a little longer and engaged in more complex play. This is called ‚Äújoint attention.‚Äù In the studies, the toddlers continued to play with their toys‚Äîeven after the parents shifted their attention away.

![](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/2022_04_07_Opal_APP3873.jpg?resize=1024%2C1024&ssl=1)

### 3 key takeaways to help increase your toddler‚Äôs attention span

**Touch, gesture, and talk about their toys.** Play alongside your toddler as they put a ball down the **Slide & Seek Ball Run**. Gesture to show them how to open the different doors and get the ball out, narrating as you play: ‚ÄúLook, here‚Äôs the ball!‚Äù

**Use action words.** One study showed that when parents used ‚Äúaction-orienting‚Äù talk‚Äî‚ÄùLet‚Äôs roll the ball!‚Äù‚Äîthe toddlers engaged in even richer and deeper play. Watch your toddler and comment on their actions or what they could do next. You could say, ‚ÄúDo you want to feed your bunny a carrot?‚Äù or ‚ÄúLet‚Äôs knock over the tower!‚Äù

**Follow your toddler‚Äôs lead.** Notice where your toddler looks or reaches and then focus on that object together. According to one of the studies, toddlers paid attention longer when their parents followed their child‚Äôs interests.

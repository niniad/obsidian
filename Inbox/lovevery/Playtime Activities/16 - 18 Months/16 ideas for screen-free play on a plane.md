## Dot sticker play

![Mother and daughter with dot stickers on their face](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Weekly_Week-77-Body-1.jpg?resize=1176%2C1176&ssl=1)

Dot stickers are simple and fun and great for developing fine motor skills. They‚Äôre also inexpensive and easy for your toddler to peel off of a sheet or roll. Bring a few sheets of [dot stickers](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B07RYZNNV3/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&tag=lovevery-20&camp=1789&creative=9325&linkCode=as2&creativeASIN=B07RYZNNV3&linkId=d0f29d2c176a6e0d9102d8dd52c35a46), some plain [white paper](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B01AW5V7PE/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&tag=lovevery-20&camp=1789&creative=9325&linkCode=as2&creativeASIN=B01AW5V7PE&linkId=cec4c23195f8648674fb092ddba3c0bd), and a sheet of [construction paper](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0013CA0LI/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&tag=lovevery-20&camp=1789&creative=9325&linkCode=as2&creativeASIN=B0013CA0LI&linkId=18caccca0cacacac5b0b8bba10ecb590) for these play ideas:

**Name the dots:** Stick the dots on different parts of your toddler‚Äôs body and your own. Play ‚Äúwhere is your nose, elbow, arm?‚Äù

**Dot hide-and-seek:** Stick a dot on yourself without your toddler seeing (on an elbow or knee, for example) and encourage them to find it.

**Dot sticker collage:** Stick dots on a big piece of white paper on the tray table. Have your toddler tear up a piece of construction paper and use the stickers to stick the pieces of torn paper to the white sheet

*Safety note: As always with small objects, supervise your toddler to make sure they don‚Äôt put the stickers in their mouth.*

## Little presents

![Child sitting in an air plane seat opening a toy](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Weekly_Week-77-Body-3.jpg?resize=1176%2C882&ssl=1)

Wrap up some new little toys for your toddler to open that you won‚Äôt be devastated to lose. You can wrap them in leftover wrapping paper or newspaper, or in little containers, bags, or small boxes. This is a great way to use up those extra scraps of odd-sized wrapping paper you have lying around if you are anything like me. Unwrapping loosely-wrapped gifts is a great fine motor activity for your toddler, allowing them to practice using both hands at once.

Ideas for inexpensive presents to entertain your baby or toddler on the plane:

**Wind-up toys:** Toddlers love to hold wind-up toys and stop the motion with their hands (they rarely want to just watch it go, and that‚Äôs okay).

**Finger puppets:** You can sing songs and play games like peek-a-boo with the puppets on your flight.

**Feathers:** Tickle each other with them, or stick them to paper with the dot stickers.

## Sticky note flags

These are fun to stick around the seats and easy to unstick (and eventually reuse) when you are done.

## Markers and paper

![Child and mother sitting together on an air plane drawing on paper](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Weekly_Week-77-Body-5.jpg?resize=1176%2C1176&ssl=1)

If you can stand the mess with washable markers, they make a mark more easily and can be more fun to draw with than crayons. If markers get on the tray table or your toddler‚Äôs hands, they can use their own travel pack of wipes to clean it off. I like to bring some blank sheets of white printer paper to color together. Coloring with your toddler makes it more meaningful and they are more likely to stay engaged longer. Child development specialists call this ‚Äúshared attention.‚Äù

You can also use one of the dot stickers to stick and display your toddler‚Äôs art on the airplane window or the seat in front of you.

## Snacks ‚Äî but more importantly, the snacks container

Snacks in packaging with open-and-close lids (like puffs) are the best kind. You can play so many games with puff containers or small raisin boxes. Some ideas:

**Puff peek-a-boo:** Hide some puffs in one of your hands and see if your toddler can guess which hand has the puffs.

**Counting games:** Line the puffs up and count them for your toddler on the tray table.

**Here comes the airplane:** If you‚Äôre up for it, your toddler will love feeding you. üôÉ

## Light switch and air blower exploration

![Child turning on the light on an airplane](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/Weekly_Week-77-Body-7.jpg?resize=1024%2C1024&ssl=1)

Have your toddler stand on your lap or lift them up to reach the light switch (not the flight attendant call button üòâ) and narrate ‚Äúlight on, light off.‚Äù My little guy said the word ‚Äúlight‚Äù for the first time while playing with the switches on an airplane and I will never forget it!

You can also twist the blower on and off and talk about how the air is blowing on their face or through their hair.

## Lap songs and games

Sing songs to your toddler while they sit on your lap or lie down across you. Clap, snap, and make the rhythms come alive‚Äîfellow passengers may just join in üé∂. [Here are lyrics to songs](https://blog.lovevery.com/child-development/lyrics-and-gestures-for-the-best-baby-play-songs-are-here/) that are great for singing and clapping along.

## Ice and plastic cups

Ask for an extra cup and pour ice from one cup to the other or slide the ice around the tray table. Talk about how the ice is cold and then, when it melts, talk about how it is turning to water. Your toddler can use the napkin to wipe up the water.

## Opening and closing the window shades

If you have a window seat, helping your toddler practice opening and closing the window shade with your hand over theirs while saying ‚Äúopen, close‚Äù is entertaining.

## An excursion to see the flight attendants

![Child talking to the flight attendant in an air plane](https://i0.wp.com/blog.lovevery.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Weekly_Week-77-Body-9.jpg?resize=1176%2C1176&ssl=1)

Sometimes flight attendants are busy and can‚Äôt take the time to engage with your toddler. But often they have a few minutes and will welcome the opportunity for a short visit.

* You can have your toddler bring their trash to the back of the plane and throw it away in the flight attendant‚Äôs trash bag.
* Your toddler can hand the attendant some of their scribble art or some dot stickers as a ‚Äúgift.‚Äù
* You can go to the back of the plane with your toddler and ask for a cup of ice together.
